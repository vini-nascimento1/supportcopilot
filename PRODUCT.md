# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Fanvue support agents and managers using an authenticated internal workspace. They work through live customer support queues, Intercom cases, Gmail, Slack, playbooks, metrics, automation, and a desktop canvas that embeds operational tools.

## Product Purpose

Fanvue Support Copilot helps agents understand cases, consult playbooks and connected knowledge, draft customer replies, and move between support tools without losing context. Success means faster, safer customer support work with clear human control over any outbound action.

## Brand Personality

Calm, practical, and precise. The interface should feel like a trustworthy operations tool: focused on the current task, transparent about system state, and restrained in visual treatment.

## Anti-references

Avoid marketing-style layouts, decorative visual effects, novelty controls, oversized typography, and anything that makes a dense support workflow feel like a landing page. Avoid hiding critical send, state, or source information behind decorative UI.

## Design Principles

- Preserve agent control over customer-facing actions.
- Keep dense workflows scannable and predictable.
- Prefer familiar product UI patterns over expressive novelty.
- Surface status, risk, and context at the point where the agent acts.
- Reuse existing tokens, components, and canvas interaction patterns.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Use standard controls, keyboard-reachable actions, visible focus states, sufficient contrast, and reduced-motion-safe interactions. Customer support workflows should remain usable under time pressure and across light or dark themes.
